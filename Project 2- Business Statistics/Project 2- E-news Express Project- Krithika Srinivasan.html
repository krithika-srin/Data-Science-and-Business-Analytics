1. Do the users spend more time on the new landing page than the old landing page?¶

Are the assumptions of the T test satisfied?
The control and treatment groups are independent of each other
The time spent on the landing page is a continuous variable
Sample std deviations are different- therefore population std deviations are unknown
Sample size > 30
5% significance interval
Test for comparing means (unknown variance) - T test

mu1 = population mean of control group
mu2 = population mean of treatment group
Old Landing Page = Control
New Landing Page = Treatment

Null hypothesis: mu1 = mu2
Alternate hypothesis: mu1 < mu2

Response- mean time spent on new page Factor- the preferred languages

Null Hypothesis: mu1 = mu2 = mu3
Alternate Hypothesis: atleast one of the means is not the same

Are the assumptions of the Anova test satisfied?
Normally distributed - Yes
Samples are independent - Yes
Population variances are equal (Assumption)
One Way Anova F Test
